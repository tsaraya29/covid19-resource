News & Announcements

IDES Offices Closed to the Public Until Further Notice

The health and safety of our customers and staff are of highest importance. In order to protect everyone through social distancing, IDES offices are closed to the public until further notice.

Individuals may access IDES services using the following methods:

File an Unemployment Insurance claim online

For questions about Unemployment Insurance, please call our Customer Service Center at 800-244-5631. Individuals with a hearing impairment who would like to speak with someone in the Claimant Services Center should dial 711 for the Illinois Relay System. Once connected, please direct the communications analyst to call IDES at 800-244-5631.

To certify for Unemployment Insurance benefits, click here or call Tele-Serve at 312-338-4337, Monday - Friday 3:00 a.m. – 7:30 p.m.

To post a resume, search job postings, or find a job, visit www.illinoisjoblink.com

All IDES Staff - Face Mask Requirement

A modified version of the state’s stay at home order will go into effect May 1, 2020. IDES will continue to remain operational as an essential governmental function while also remaining closed to the public. However, the new executive order includes an important modification regarding Face Coverings. Beginning on May 1, individuals will be required to wear a face-covering or a mask when in a public place where they cannot maintain a six-foot social distance.

Upon entering any IDES office, you will be required to wear a face-covering or mask. You are to also adhere to any specific guidelines that may be posted in your worksite concerning social distancing.

When moving around the office (for example, to the copier or bathroom) please wear a face-covering or mask since it is impractical to maintain 6 feet of physical distance between yourself and others. While sitting at your workstation, you will not be required to wear a face-covering or mask since IDES cubicles and offices are at least six feet apart.

If you need a mask, please ask your supervisor. IDES will continue to provide disposable masks to employees reporting to work. Please see attached Guidance on the Use of Masks by the General Public.

As public health experts continue to learn about COVID-19, these steps are being implemented as a preventive measure for the safety and well-being of all employees. Thank you for your continued cooperation.

Information for Claimants

Lost Wages Assistance Information and FAQs

9/4/20 – On August 28, 2020, Illinois submitted its application to the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) for the new federal Lost Wages Assistance (LWA) funds to provide supplemental payments to unemployment benefits as a result of COVID-19. This program was established by a presidential memorandum.

LWA will provide eligible claimants an additional $300 per week. According to the presidential memorandum, the program prohibits claimants with less than a $100 weekly benefit (including the amount received for a dependent) from receiving these additional funds.

LWA is a completely separate program from the Federal Pandemic Unemployment Compensation Program (FPUC), which provided an additional $600 per week to all eligible claimants until its expiration on July 25, 2020. Please see the Frequently Asked Questions about the Lost Wages Assistance Program

Twenty Weeks of Extended Regular State Benefits Now Available;
Extended Benefits Available in Addition to PEUC as a Result of Unemployment Rate

8/6/20 – IDES announced 20 weeks of state extended benefits (EB) are now available to those who exhaust the allotted 26 weeks of regular state unemployment and the additional 13 weeks of federal Pandemic Emergency Unemployment Compensation (PEUC) benefits. Illinois law provides for 20 weeks of EB during times of “high unemployment,” as defined in the law, instead of the 13 weeks of EB that was available in Illinois since May.
